Admissions

Join our community of learners dedicated to faith, service, and excellence

Welcome

Begin Your Journey

St. Mary's University College welcomes all eligible South Sudanese citizens who wish to pursue quality higher education grounded in Catholic values. Our admissions process is designed to identify students who will thrive in our academic community and contribute to South Sudan's development.

We offer programs in Rehabilitation Sciences, Education, and Human Development—each designed to prepare you for a meaningful career serving your community and nation.

Requirements

Admission Requirements

Academic Requirements

  • South Sudan Secondary School Certificate Examination (SSCSE) or equivalent
  • Minimum required grades in relevant subjects
  • English language proficiency
  • Science subjects for Rehabilitation Sciences applicants

Required Documents

  • Completed application form
  • Original academic certificates and transcripts
  • National ID or birth certificate
  • Four passport-size photographs
  • Letter of recommendation
  • Medical fitness certificate
How to Apply

Application Process

Step 1: Obtain Application Form

Application forms are available at the Admissions Office on campus or can be requested by contacting us.

Step 2: Complete Application

Fill out the application form completely and accurately. Incomplete applications will not be processed.

Step 3: Pay Application Fee

Pay the non-refundable application fee at the Finance Office. Keep your receipt for submission.

Step 4: Submit Application

Submit your completed application with all required documents to the Admissions Office before the deadline.

Step 5: Await Decision

Applications are reviewed by the Admissions Committee. Successful applicants will be notified of their acceptance.

Financial Information

Tuition & Fees

St. Mary's University College strives to make quality education accessible. Tuition and fees vary by program. For current fee schedules and payment options, please contact the Finance Office.

Fees Include

  • Tuition fees (per semester)
  • Registration fee
  • Library fee
  • Laboratory fee (where applicable)
  • Examination fee
Contact Finance Office

Financial Assistance

Limited financial assistance may be available for qualified students demonstrating financial need. Students are encouraged to inquire about scholarship opportunities and payment plans at the Finance Office.

External scholarships from government agencies, NGOs, and religious organizations may also be available. The Admissions Office can provide guidance on external funding opportunities.

Student Experience

Campus Life

Accommodation

Information about on-campus and off-campus housing options is available through the Student Affairs Office. We can assist students in finding suitable accommodation.

Spiritual Life

As a Catholic institution, we offer opportunities for spiritual growth including Mass, prayer groups, and retreats. Students of all faiths are welcome and respected.

Student Support

Academic advising, counseling services, and peer support are available to help students succeed in their studies and personal development.
