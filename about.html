About St. Mary's University College

Discover our history, mission, and commitment to transforming South Sudan through education

Our Story

A History of Hope

St. Mary's University College was founded in 2008 and officially inaugurated on April 15, 2009, as a collaborative effort between the Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Juba, the South Sudanese Ministry of Gender, Social Welfare and Religious Affairs, and OVCI la Nostra Famiglia—an Italian Catholic NGO that has been working in South Sudan since 1983.

The university emerged from the Catholic Church's long-standing commitment to education in South Sudan. Even during the decades of civil war, the Church maintained schools and provided education to communities across the region. St. Mary's represents the culmination of this dedication—a higher education institution designed to train professionals who can lead South Sudan's development.

Located on the Usratuna campus in Juba, the university opened its doors with the first cohort of 13 students in the College of Rehabilitation Sciences. Today, we continue to grow, adding new faculties and programs to meet the evolving needs of our nation.

Our Partners

Founded Through Collaboration

Three organizations united by a shared vision for South Sudan's future

Archdiocese of Juba

The Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Juba has been a pillar of education, healthcare, and social services in South Sudan for generations. The late Archbishop Paulino Lukudu Loro served as the founding Vice Chancellor, providing spiritual and administrative leadership to the university.

OVCI la Nostra Famiglia

The Volunteers' Organization for International Co-operation (OVCI) is an Italian Catholic NGO that has been continuously working in South Sudan since 1983. They provide technical expertise, funding, and management support, particularly in rehabilitation sciences.

Government of South Sudan

The Ministry of Gender, Social Welfare and Religious Affairs partnered in establishing the university, recognizing the critical need for trained social workers and rehabilitation professionals to serve South Sudan's vulnerable populations.

Our Purpose

Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To train a new generation of men and women who can take responsibility for the future of South Sudan, equipping them with knowledge, professional skills, and Christian values to serve their communities with excellence and compassion.

Our Vision

To be a center of academic excellence and human development, producing graduates who will lead the reconstruction and development of South Sudan in healthcare, education, and social services.

Our Values

  • Faith-based education rooted in Catholic tradition
  • Service to the most vulnerable in society
  • Academic excellence and professional integrity
  • Commitment to peace, justice, and reconciliation
  • Respect for human dignity and diversity
Recognition

Accreditation & Affiliation

Government Recognition

St. Mary's University College is officially recognized by the Ministry of Education, Science and Technology of South Sudan. Our programs lead to officially recognized higher education degrees including bachelor's degrees in several areas of study.

Catholic Affiliation

As a Catholic institution of higher learning, we operate under the guidance of the Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Juba. Our education integrates faith formation with academic excellence, preparing students for lives of service and leadership.

Administration

University Leadership

Guided by dedicated leaders committed to our mission

Office of the Vice Chancellor

The university is led by the Vice Chancellor, who provides overall administrative and academic leadership. In accordance with Catholic tradition, the Archbishop of Juba serves as the Chancellor of the university, providing spiritual guidance and oversight.

Founding Vice Chancellor:
The late Archbishop Paulino Lukudu Loro (1940-2021) served as the founding Vice Chancellor, guiding the university through its formative years with wisdom and dedication.
